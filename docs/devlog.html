How does our engine work?

RayCasting, is a technique that creates a "3D-like view" from a "2D map". Rays are cast from the player`s position in the viewing direction, and each ray stops when it hits a wall in the 2D grid. The distance to the wall is then used to calculate the height of a vertical slice on the screen. By drawing these slices side by side, a scene appears three-dimensional without using a true 3D world.
